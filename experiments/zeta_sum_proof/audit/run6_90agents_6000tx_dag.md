## Key Findings

### 1. OMEGA REACHED — 7B Model Proves ζ Regularization
- **First proof by a weak model** in TuringOS history. Prior Run 2 (DeepSeek-V3+R1) proved it; now Qwen2.5-7B does too.
- Proof quality is LOW: Golden Path steps 2-15 are essentially the same sentence ("Express S(N) as the sum...") repeated with minor variations. The model brute-forced to [COMPLETE] by depth, not by mathematical insight.
- **But the architecture delivered**: 90 agents collaboratively built an 18-step chain across 38 generations and 50 minutes of rate-limited operation.

### 2. Budget Confirms the Scaling Law
```
Agents  Tx/agent  Depth   Proved?
15      20        11      NO
30      10         9      NO
90      3.3        5      NO
90      19        18      YES ✓
```
- At ~20 tx/agent, depth ≈ 11-18 and proof is possible.
- At ~3 tx/agent, depth collapses to 5 and proof fails.
- **Rule of thumb**: `tx_budget ≥ agents × 20` for meaningful proof search.

### 3. Bear Role — FINALLY NET BEAR at Scale
- Bear: 157 YES / 186 NO → **net BEAR** for the first time at 90 agents with sufficient budget.
- Run 5 (90/300): Bears were net BULL (34Y > 26N) — not enough turns to find flaws.
- Run 6 (90/6000): More turns → Bears find targets → genuine shorting.
- **tx_293_by_29**: 52 bets (!), 7126Y vs 5708N — most contested node in TuringOS history. A Math node that attracted massive bull/bear warfare.

- **Implication**: investment prompt should show price-sorted nodes, not just recent ones.
